# Stakeholder Alignment

## When to use
- A recommendation needs approval from people who don't yet agree (or don't yet know).
- A reorg, reallocation, or strategy shift will create winners and losers.
- The decision meeting is coming and you want it pre-decided, not a live debate you might lose.

## When not to use
- The content/story isn't sharp yet → use `narrative-builder` first.
- You just need the written artifact → use `decision-memo`.

## Method
1. **Map stakeholders on influence × interest.** Place each decision-maker and key influencer. High-influence/high-interest = manage closely; high-influence/low-interest = the swing votes you must activate; the rest get kept-informed. Identify the **economic buyer** (who controls the resource) and the **blockers**.
2. **Write a motivation analysis per key player.** What they *want* (their objective, their metrics, their incentives), what they *fear* (what this costs them — budget, headcount, status, risk), and their current stance (champion / supporter / neutral / skeptic / blocker). People oppose what threatens them, not what's wrong on the merits.
3. **Design an engagement card per swing vote.** The tailored message (framed in *their* terms, addressing *their* fear), the right channel (1:1 vs group — never surprise a powerful skeptic in a group), the messenger they trust, and the timing.
4. **Sequence the pre-wire.** Engage supporters first (build momentum and air cover), then neutrals, then skeptics 1:1 *before* the meeting — never let a skeptic hear it first in the room. By the meeting, every vote is known.
5. **Pre-mortem the decision meeting.** Who could blow it up, with what objection, and the pre-agreed defuse (often: the objection has already been handled 1:1 with that person beforehand).

## Decision rules
- Count the votes before the meeting. If you don't know how each swing vote will land, you are not ready to convene.
- Never surprise a powerful skeptic in public — they will dig in to save face. Convert or neutralize 1:1 first.
- Frame every message in the stakeholder's own interest and metrics, not the merits of your analysis. Being right is necessary, not sufficient.

## Output format
1. **Stakeholder map** — influence × interest grid with each player placed; economic buyer and blockers flagged.
2. **Motivation analysis** — per key player: wants / fears / current stance.
3. **Engagement plan** — per swing vote: message, channel, messenger, timing.
4. **Pre-wire sequence** — the order of conversations leading up to the decision.
5. **Meeting pre-mortem** — likely disruptions and the pre-agreed defuse for each.

## Quality bar
- Messages are tailored to each stakeholder's wants/fears, not one generic pitch.
- A real *sequence* of pre-meeting conversations — alignment is engineered, not hoped for.
- The economic buyer and blockers are explicitly named.
- The plan reflects organizational reality (politics, face, history), not an org chart.

## Common failure modes
- Relying on the strength of the analysis and walking into the meeting cold.
- One-size-fits-all messaging that addresses no one's actual fear.
- Surprising a senior skeptic in the room and triggering public entrenchment.

## Pairs with
Follows `narrative-builder` (the story to pre-wire) and precedes the `decision-memo` / decision meeting. Essential alongside `operating-model-design` and `cost-transformation` (reorgs and cuts are intensely political).

## Worked example
Input: a contested reorg moving budget from a powerful regional GM to a new central function. Map: the CFO is the economic buyer (neutral, fears losing cost control), the regional GM is the blocker (high influence, will lose headcount), two other GMs are swing votes. Motivation: the GM fears status/headcount loss more than the strategy; reframe the central function as *amplifying* their numbers, and protect their title. Pre-wire: secure the CEO and one supportive GM first, give the CFO the cost-control guardrails 1:1, then meet the blocking GM privately a week ahead with a face-saving role offer. By the meeting all four swing/blocker stances are known and the GM's objection has already been handled offline.
